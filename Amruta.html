Hospital

Manage internal hospital facilities including patient records, available beds, doctors, and emergency services.

Algorithms / DSA Used:
  • Priority Queues – to assign patients based on urgency
  • Hash Tables / Maps – to manage patient records

Waste Collection Route Optimization

• Plans garbage truck path covering max junctions
• Finds reachable areas + reduces fuel/time

Algorithms Used: DFS + Minimum Spanning Tree

Water & Electricity

Monitor water supply, quality, and electricity distribution for better resource management.

Algorithms / DSA Used:
  • Dynamic Allocation – to distribute water/electricity efficiently
  • Priority Queues – for critical area supply
  • Graphs – to represent pipeline and grid networks

Parking & Petrol

Locate available parking spaces and nearby petrol/EV stations for efficient traffic and fuel management.

Algorithms / DSA Used:
  • BFS + Min-Heap – to find the nearest available parking or fuel station
  • Hash Maps – to maintain parking slot availability

Salt Production

Monitor and optimize salt harvesting, production, and distribution efficiently.

Algorithms / DSA Used:
  • Priority Queue (Max-Heap) – harvest pans with highest salt quantity first
  • Graphs / Shortest Path – for optimal delivery route planning

Relation With Data Structures & Algorithms (DAA)

    Complexity & Efficiency

    • Priority Queue :insertion/removal (used for patient assignment, salt harvesting, and critical utilities)
    • Hash Map :lookup (managing patient records, parking availability)
    • DFS :waste collection route optimization
    • BFS :finding nearest parking or petrol stations
    • Graphs :for traversal, O(E log V) for MST (city layout, pipelines, and waste routes)
    • Min-Heap :nearest parking/fuel station search

Flowcharts & City Maps

Complexity & Efficiency

    Complexity & Efficiency

    • Priority Queue → O(log n) insertion/removal (used for patient assignment, salt harvesting, and critical utilities)
    • Hash Map → O(1) lookup (managing patient records, parking availability)
    • DFS → O(V + E) (waste collection route optimization)
    • BFS → O(V + E) (finding nearest parking or petrol stations)
    • Graphs → O(V + E) for traversal, O(E log V) for MST (city layout, pipelines, and waste routes)
    • Min-Heap → O(log n) (nearest parking/fuel station search)

    Conclusion

    Overall, the integration of priority queues, hash maps, graphs, and other DAA techniques transforms urban management into a smarter, faster, and more sustainable system, improving both city operations and citizen quality of life.
