ðŸ”¶ Smart Emergency Response Control Centre (SERCC)

The Smart Emergency Response Control Centre is the heart of the emergency ecosystem. It acts as a unified digital platform that coordinates ambulance services, fire departments, and police units. The system continuously monitors the city and allocates the nearest available vehicle for every emergency call.

  • Real-time vehicle tracking using GPS.
  • Predicts response time using live traffic conditions.
  • Automated rerouting if roads are blocked or under construction.
  • Central dashboard shows availability of all emergency units.
  • Historical data helps improve future emergency planning.

Impact: Reduces response time, improves transparency, and increases efficiency during peak emergency hours.

ðŸ”¶ Disaster Evacuation & Safe Shelter Planning

Cities must be prepared for floods, fires, and industrial accidents. This system uses simulations and graph-based algorithms to identify safe evacuation routes and guide people to shelter zones during danger.

  • Uses BFS/DFS to find reachable shelters.
  • Identifies bottlenecks and choke points in evacuation roads.
  • Predicts disaster outcomes using historical patterns.
  • Provides automated alerts for high-risk zones.

Impact: Saves lives during disasters and increases city resilience.

ðŸ”¶ Integrated Fire Hazard Monitoring & Rapid Response

Fires can spread rapidly and require immediate action. This subsystem integrates fire sensors, hydrant maps, and routing engines to ensure the fastest dispatch of fire trucks.

  • Instant alerts from fire detection sensors.
  • Shows nearest hydrants and fire stations.
  • Considers road width & slope for fire truck routing.
  • Predicts fire spread using wind and weather data.

Impact: Reduces fire damage, prevents spread, and speeds up response time.
