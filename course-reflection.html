Section 2 – Course Reflection

These reflections summarize my learning experience throughout the Design & Analysis of Algorithms course. Each response represents how my understanding evolved as we progressed from foundations to applying algorithms in our Smart City project.

1. Most Impactful Concept Learned

The most impactful concept for me was understanding how time and space complexity influence real-world performance. Earlier I focused only on writing code that works, but this course showed me why an efficient solution matters far more in real applications. Choosing the right data structure can change an algorithm from slow to lightning-fast.

2. How My Perspective on Algorithms Evolved

At the beginning, algorithms felt like complicated procedures to memorize. But as the course progressed, I realized that algorithms are really structured ways of thinking. They help break large problems into logical steps and guide efficient decision-making. This mindset shift changed the way I solve any technical problem now.

3. Challenges Faced During the Project

The biggest challenge was selecting the right algorithm for each subsystem of our Smart Emergency Response System. We constantly compared BFS, Dijkstra, Segment Trees, and hashing to justify every choice. This forced me not just to use algorithms but to truly understand their purpose and their efficiency.

4. How the Team Project Strengthened My Learning

Working in a team gave me a deeper understanding of algorithmic problem-solving. Everyone had different ideas, and discussing them made concepts clearer. We evaluated trade-offs, efficiency, and feasibility — exactly how real engineering teams operate.

5. A Concept I Initially Struggled With

I initially struggled with segment trees, especially how queries and updates work in logarithmic time. But once I implemented them and linked the idea to real-world systems (like crime frequency queries), it became one of my favorite concepts from the course.

6. Skills I Gained From This Course

The most valuable skill I gained is the ability to analyze and justify algorithmic choices. I can now evaluate time complexity, consider constraints, and design solutions that scale. This analytical thinking will help me not only in academics but also in interviews, projects, and real software development.
