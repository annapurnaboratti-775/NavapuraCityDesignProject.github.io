Section 2 – Course Reflection

These questions reflect how my understanding of algorithms evolved throughout the course and how the project strengthened my problem-solving approach.

1. Most Impactful Concept Learned

The most impactful concept for me was understanding how time and space complexity influence real-world performance. Earlier I focused only on making programs run, but this course taught me why efficient algorithms matter. Choosing the right data structure can turn a slow solution into a scalable one.

2. How My Perspective on Algorithms Evolved

At first, algorithms felt like long steps to memorize. Over time, I understood that algorithms are actually ways of thinking. They break problems into logical components and guide us to the most efficient solution. This mindset shift changed the way I solve technical problems now.

3. Challenges Faced During the Project

The most challenging part was choosing the right algorithm for each subsystem of the Smart Emergency Response System. We constantly compared BFS, Dijkstra, Segment Trees, and Hashing to justify our decisions. This challenge made me truly understand how algorithms behave in real-world conditions.

4. How the Team Project Strengthened My Learning

Working as a team helped me understand different perspectives on the same problem. We debated algorithmic choices, efficiency, and design alternatives — exactly how real engineering teams work. This teamwork deepened my understanding more than studying alone.

5. A Concept I Initially Struggled With

Segment Trees were difficult for me at first — especially understanding how updates and queries run in O(log n) time. But once I implemented them and connected them to real scenarios like crime-frequency queries, the concept became clearer and more intuitive.

6. Skills I Gained From This Course

The most valuable skill I gained is the ability to evaluate and justify algorithmic decisions. I can now analyze time complexity, consider constraints, and choose scalable solutions confidently. This skill will help me in interviews, projects, and real-world development.
