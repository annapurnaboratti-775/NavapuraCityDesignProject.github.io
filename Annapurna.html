About UIONC

UIONC is a centralized operations center that collects streams of structured and unstructured data from sensors, department databases and citizen inputs — then processes this information using robust data structures and algorithms to supply accurate, timely decisions to city officials and frontline teams.

Why it matters

Large cities often underperform not due to lack of assets but due to poor coordination. UIONC fixes information fragmentation — leading to faster emergency response, efficient resource use, and better planning.

Design Principles

  • Modularity & incremental rollout
  • Use proven algorithms, not buzzword AI
  • Government-friendly operations & low maintenance
  • Privacy-first data governance

Architecture

UIONC is organised into five practical layers — each mapping directly to well-understood data structures and algorithmic approaches.

1. Data Collection Layer

Sensors (pollution, water, power), GPS from vehicles, market feeds, department databases, CCTV logs and citizen reports form the raw inputs.

2. Data Integration & Indexing

Heterogeneous data is cleaned, normalized and indexed. Hash tables, tries and balanced trees provide fast lookups for addresses, assets and records.

3. Processing & Algorithms

Graph algorithms (shortest paths, connectivity), segment trees (zone analytics), heaps (priority dispatch) and sorting are used here for decision-making.

4. Decision Support

Dashboards, alerting engines and automated workflows translate algorithmic outputs into human-actionable steps.

5. City Operations & Public Interface

Dispatch systems, traffic control, utility crews and citizen apps receive coordinated instructions. Everything is logged for audit and planning.

Key Use Cases

Emergency Dispatch (Ambulance)

Call enters UIONC → priority assigned (heap) → best available ambulance selected (Dijkstra on live traffic graph) → route pushed to driver and traffic lights adapt to clear the way.

Flood Warning & Evacuation

Rain sensors + historical models trigger zone alerts. Segment trees provide zone severity; evacuation corridors computed with multi-source shortest path and coordinated with transport module.

Agriculture Logistics

Market price feeds + storage availability help optimize shipments. Graph algorithms compute least-cost routes, while queues manage loading docks and trucks.

Power Outage Detection

Smart meters report anomalies. Union-find identifies affected network components; crews are scheduled using priority heaps and dispatch workflows.
